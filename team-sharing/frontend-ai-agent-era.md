# Frontend Identity & Value in the AI Agent Era

## Foreword

AI Agent is fundamentally changing how software is built. As frontend developers, how do we reposition ourselves, re-evaluate our value, and continue creating impact in our teams and companies? Here are some thoughts.

---

## 1. Repositioning: From Executor to Boundary Expander

### What Is a "Super Individual"

With AI Agents, everyone can work more efficiently and amplify their professional capabilities. But do "super individuals" really exist? As of now, no company (or very few) truly has such individuals.

I define a **super individual** as: **someone who continuously expands their capability boundaries, can directly face operational needs, and independently complete the full closed loop from requirements analysis, product design, development, deployment, to launch feedback.** Eventually, they influence more people to join this workflow, forming a super team.

### Traditional Workflow vs. AI Workflow

Many companies (including ours) have the capability for this, but sometimes parallel development is needed to shorten delivery cycles — that's the core logic of traditional workflows.

What I'm still unclear about: if we fully transition to a modern AI workflow (one person = one production line), once the team is familiar and stable, would overall delivery actually accelerate? And whether this suits every company.

> **But even without such an environment, everyone should think about expanding their capability boundaries.**

---

## 2. Re-evaluating Value: Judgment & Experience Are Irreplaceable

### What AI Can't Replace

I don't believe someone without development experience or years of frontend expertise would do better work just by using AI Agents. The reason is simple:

- They lack the **judgment** and necessary **domain knowledge**
- Technology selection, project experience, engineering architecture — someone without experience wouldn't know where to start
- Based on my experience, AI still falls short in these areas; experienced guidance is still essential

The key is guidance from people with **up-to-date experience**.

### Tech Debt & Quality

Often, inexperienced users produce something that "seems to work" with AI, but maintainability, readability, and robustness suffer. Technical debt and legacy baggage accumulate over time.

### Irreplaceable Frontend Value

Frontend developers should double down on these areas — this is where our core value lies:

- **Product Sense** — intuition about product experience
- **User Experience** — attention to interaction details and polish
- **Understanding User Needs** — truly grasping what users want
- **Performance** — continuously optimizing load speed and rendering efficiency

These are what make a product truly great, and the best demonstration of our value.

---

## 3. Creating Greater Value: Efficiency, Workflow & Cost

To generate more impact in the team and company, we need to think from multiple dimensions:

1. **Improve efficiency** — how to leverage AI tools to boost productivity in daily work
2. **Optimize workflows** — not just development, but also team collaboration processes
3. **Reduce costs** — think from the company's perspective on how to lower various operational costs

These are not just technical problems — they're about mindset. Thinking proactively and driving change amplifies individual value within the team.

---

## Summary

In the AI Agent era, the frontend role isn't diminished — it needs to be redefined. Instead of worrying about being replaced, we should:

- Actively expand capability boundaries, moving toward the "super individual" ideal
- Deepen our judgment and product thinking, protecting our core value
- Think multi-dimensionally about creating greater value for the team and company

Rather than fearing AI will replace us, focus on harnessing AI to become stronger.
